TL;DR

Lightweight reactions are a staple element for user interaction within the Facebook ecosystem.  Particularly, Lightweight Reactions (LWR) for FB Stories was launched to 100% in November 2018 and have gone through several iteration while aligning closely with the current experience of Newsfeed.

We have recently introduced a new reaction (care) into the LWR ecosystem.   This has shed light on interesting observation across usage patterns and raising questions about (1) the effect of LWR ordering and (2) how aligned the stories UFI should be with NF.

Our observations show that the LWR usage behaviors between NF and Stories differ significantly.  Particularly the  like reaction is used at a much higher rate in NF while the Love reaction is more prominent in Stories.   A similar trend is experienced in IG where heart eyes emoji perform significantly stronger in IG's Quick Replies.  Additionally, the care reaction has had a negative impact on reactions in general.

Hypothesis / Test

We are proposing to test the ordering of lightweight reactions in the UFI.  We would specifically like to test the following
  • Shift the Love reaction to the first position 
  • Shift the Care reaction to the X position
    • proposal: positions 4 and 5
The hypothesis is that LWR positioning has a direct impact on usage and positioning should be optimized for the stories product.

Key Questions
  • Which reaction should be first? Like or Heart?
  • Do Heart and Care ‘conflict’ with each other?
  • Does the ordering of ‘hidden’ LWR (4th position onwards) matter?


Design

Key Points and observations 
  • The current ufi follows the same ordering of Newsfeed
  • LWR are accessed by H-scroll and many reactions are hidden
